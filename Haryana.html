Haryana

Lohri

Celebrated in January, Lohri marks the end of winter and the start of the harvest season. People light bonfires, sing traditional songs, and dance around the fire, celebrating the harvest of rabi crops.

Baisakhi

Observed in April, Baisakhi is a harvest festival that marks the beginning of the new agricultural season. It is also significant in Sikhism as the day when the Khalsa was formed. People celebrate with folk dances like Bhangra and Gidda.

Holi

Holi, celebrated in March, is the festival of colors, marking the arrival of spring. In Haryana, people celebrate by throwing colored powders, singing, dancing, and lighting bonfires, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.
