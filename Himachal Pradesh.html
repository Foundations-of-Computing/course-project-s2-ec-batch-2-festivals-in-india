Kullu Dussehra

Kullu Dussehra, celebrated in September or October, is the most famous and unique festival of Himachal Pradesh. Unlike the traditional Dussehra celebrations elsewhere, Kullu Dussehra is celebrated in a distinct way. It involves a grand procession where the idol of Lord Raghunath is paraded through the streets of Kullu. People from nearby villages gather to celebrate, and the festivities last for seven days, featuring folk dances, music, and various cultural performances. It is one of the largest festivals in the region and attracts a large number of tourists

Lohri

Lohri, celebrated in January, marks the end of the winter season and the beginning of the harvest season in Himachal Pradesh. It is particularly celebrated in the Kangra and Mandi regions, where people light bonfires, sing traditional songs, and dance around the flames. The festival is a time of celebration and thanksgiving for the harvest, and it brings communities together to enjoy the warmth and joy of the season.

Baisakhi

Baisakhi, celebrated in April, marks the start of the harvest season in Himachal Pradesh. The festival is dedicated to thanking the gods for the harvest of wheat, barley, and other crops. Farmers celebrate by performing traditional dances like "Nati," offering prayers, and sharing festive meals. It is also a time when people seek blessings for prosperity in the coming year.
