Jharkhand

Tusu (Tusu Parab)

Tusu, celebrated in December or January, is one of the most important and unique festivals in Jharkhand, especially among the tribal communities. It marks the harvest season and is dedicated to the worship of the Tusu goddess, who is believed to bring prosperity. Young girls perform traditional dances around the Tusu tree and celebrate the good harvest. The festival is characterized by singing, dancing, and the offering of traditional foods like "Pitha" (rice cakes).

Sarhul

Sarhul, celebrated in March or April, is a festival celebrated by the tribal people of Jharkhand. It marks the arrival of spring and is a thanksgiving festival to honor the earth and nature. The festival is marked by the worship of trees, especially sal trees, and features traditional dances, songs, and rituals. People wear traditional attire and celebrate with family and community feasts.

Karam Festival

Karam, celebrated in August or September, is a tribal festival that honors the Karam tree, believed to bring prosperity and good fortune. The festival is marked by traditional rituals, including offering prayers to the Karam tree and performing dances in its honor. It is celebrated with great enthusiasm, especially by the Oraon and Munda tribes of Jharkhand.
