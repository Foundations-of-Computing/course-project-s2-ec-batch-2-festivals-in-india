Kerala

Onam

Onam is the most important festival of Kerala, celebrated in August or September. It marks the homecoming of the legendary King Mahabali and is celebrated with grand feasts (Onasadya), flower decorations (Pookalam), traditional dance (Thiruvathira), and the famous Vallamkali (snake boat race).

Vishu

Vishu, celebrated in April, marks the Malayali New Year. The highlight of the festival is the "Vishukkani," an auspicious arrangement of items like rice, fruits, flowers, and a lit lamp, which is the first thing people see upon waking. Firecrackers, feasts, and new clothes add to the celebrations.

Theyyam Festival

Theyyam Festival, celebrated from December to April in temples across North Kerala, is a unique ritualistic art form where performers, dressed as deities, enact mythological stories. It is an integral part of the culture in districts like Kannur and Kasaragod.

Eid & Ramadan

Kerala has a significant Muslim population, and Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha are celebrated with special prayers at mosques, community feasts, and charity. Ramadan is observed with fasting, and markets come alive in the evenings with delicious food.
