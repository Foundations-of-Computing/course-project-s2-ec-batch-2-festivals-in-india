Manipur

Yaoshang (Holi)

February or March (based on the lunar calendar). Yaoshang is the Manipuri version of Holi and is one of the most important festivals in Manipur. It marks the arrival of spring and is celebrated with colorful dances, songs, and throwing of colored powders. People also engage in traditional rituals and sports, such as the famous Yaoshang fair and "Thabal Chongba" (a traditional Manipuri dance).

Lai Haraoba

May or June (depending on the lunar calender). This festival is dedicated to the worship of the gods and goddesses of the Meitei community. It includes rituals, prayers, dances, and theatrical performances. The festival is marked by offering worship to the deities to ensure the well-being and prosperity of the community.

Ningol Chakouba

October or November (based on the lunar calendar). Ningol Chakouba is a traditional festival celebrated by the Meitei community. It is a family festival where married women return to their parental homes, and a grand feast is held to celebrate familial bonds. The day is marked with traditional meals, gifts, and joyous celebrations.

Cheiraoba

April (usually in the first half of the month).Cheiraoba is the Manipuri New Year festival. It is a time for families to clean their homes, prepare special dishes, and offer prayers to ensure prosperity in the coming year. The festival also involves processions and community gatherings, and people ascend nearby hills to celebrate with traditional rituals.
