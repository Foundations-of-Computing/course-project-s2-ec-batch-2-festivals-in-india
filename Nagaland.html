Nagaland

Hornbill Festival

The Hornbill Festival, celebrated in December, is the most famous and vibrant festival of Nagaland. Held annually in Kohima, it is a week-long celebration of Naga culture, featuring traditional dances, songs, art exhibitions, and sports. It brings together various Naga tribes to showcase their unique customs, handicrafts, and traditional attire. The festival is named after the hornbill bird, which holds cultural significance in many Naga tribes.

Sekrenyi Festival

Sekrenyi, celebrated in February by the Angami tribe, is a spring festival marking the beginning of the farming season. The festival is a time of purification and renewal, with rituals for health and prosperity. It includes feasting, traditional dances, and a variety of community celebrations, such as the "Lao," a procession of men dressed in traditional attire.

Mimkut Festival

Mimkut, celebrated in September, is a unique festival of the Zeliangrong Naga tribe in Nagaland. It is a harvest festival celebrated to honor the spirits of ancestors and seek blessings for the next season's crops. The festival is characterized by traditional dances, music, feasting, and prayers for prosperity. During Mimkut, people perform rituals at the village shrine and offer sacrifices to ensure the well-being of the community.
