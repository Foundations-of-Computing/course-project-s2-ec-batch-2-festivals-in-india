Odisha

Nuakhai

Nuakhai, celebrated in August or September, is a unique and significant festival in Odisha, especially among the people of Western Odisha. It marks the first harvest of the season and is celebrated with immense joy. The festival involves offering the newly harvested rice (Nua) to the deities and partaking in a feast with family and friends. Nuakhai is a time for community gatherings, cultural performances, and prayers for prosperity in the coming year.

Maha Shivaratri

Maha Shivaratri, celebrated in February or March, is an important religious festival in Odisha dedicated to Lord Shiva. Devotees observe fasts, night vigils, and prayers at temples like the Lingaraj Temple in Bhubaneswar, and participate in processions and rituals.

Rath Yatra

Rath Yatra, held in July or August, is one of the most famous and grand festivals of Odisha. Celebrated in Puri, it marks the journey of Lord Jagannath, Lord Balabhadra, and Goddess Subhadra from the Jagannath Temple to the Gundicha Temple in massive, colorfully decorated chariots. Devotees pull the chariots in a huge procession.
