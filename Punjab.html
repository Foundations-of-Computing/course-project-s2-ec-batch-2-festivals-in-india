Punjab

Guru Nanak Jayanti

Guru Nanak Jayanti, also known as Gurpurab, celebrates the birth anniversary of Guru Nanak Dev, the founder of Sikhism. It is observed with great devotion in Punjab, especially in Amritsar. The celebrations include Akhand Path (continuous reading of the Guru Granth Sahib), Nagar Kirtan processions, and langar at Gurudwaras. The Golden Temple is beautifully illuminated, making it a mesmerizing sight.

Hola Mohalla

Hola Mohalla, celebrated the day after Holi, is a unique Sikh festival that showcases martial arts, mock battles, and horseback riding skills. It was started by Guru Gobind Singh to promote bravery and discipline among the Sikhs. The main celebrations take place at Anandpur Sahib, where thousands of Sikhs participate in grand processions, religious gatherings, and community feasts.

Baisakhi

Baisakhi, celebrated on April 13 or 14, is the Punjabi New Year and an important harvest festival. It also holds great significance for Sikhs as it marks the formation of the Khalsa by Guru Gobind Singh in 1699. People celebrate with fairs, processions, and traditional folk dances like Bhangra. Gurudwaras are beautifully decorated, and devotees offer prayers and take part in community feasts (langar).
