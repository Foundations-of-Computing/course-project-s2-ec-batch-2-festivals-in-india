Rajasthan

Gangaur

Gangaur, celebrated in March or April, is one of the most popular festivals in Rajasthan, especially for women. It honors Goddess Parvati and celebrates the union of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. Women dress in traditional attire, sing songs, and carry beautiful idols of the goddess in processions.

Marwar Festival

The Marwar Festival, celebrated in September or October, is a unique cultural festival of Rajasthan that honors the region's heroes, particularly those from the Marwar region (Jodhpur). The festival is a grand celebration of folk music, dance, and traditional performances, showcasing the vibrant cultural heritage of Rajasthan. It includes camel rides, traditional folk music performances, dances like Ghoomar and Kalbeliya, and various competitions. The festival also features exhibitions of local handicrafts and cuisine, making it a great showcase of Rajasthan's culture.

Diwali

Diwali, celebrated in October or November, is one of the most important festivals in Rajasthan. The festival of lights is observed with great enthusiasm, where homes are decorated with oil lamps (diyas), rangolis, and colorful lights. People burst crackers, exchange sweets, and worship Goddess Lakshmi for wealth and prosperity.
