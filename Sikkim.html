Sikkim

Losar

Losar, celebrated in February or March, is the Tibetan New Year and one of the most important festivals in Sikkim. It marks the beginning of a new year in the lunar calendar. The celebrations include offerings to deities, traditional dances, songs, and a festive mood in the entire region. It is a time for family reunions, feasting, and prayers for prosperity in the coming year.

Chhewar Ceremony

The Chhewar Ceremony, celebrated by the Hindu community in Sikkim, marks the coming-of-age of young boys. It is similar to the sacred thread ceremony or "Upanayana" in other parts of India. The festival is celebrated with family prayers, traditional rituals, and a festive gathering of friends and relatives. It is an important cultural and religious event for the community.

Lhosar

Lhosar, celebrated by the Bhutia and Lepcha communities, is the celebration of the New Year according to the Sikkimese lunar calendar. This festival, celebrated in February or March, is similar to the Tibetan New Year (Losar) and includes traditional dances, feasting, and religious ceremonies. People wear new clothes, exchange gifts, and take part in community celebrations.
