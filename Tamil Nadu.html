Tamil Nadu

Pongal

Pongal, celebrated in January, is the most important festival in Tamil Nadu. It is a harvest festival that lasts for four days and marks the beginning of the sun's journey towards the northern hemisphere. The festival includes cooking the traditional dish "Pongal" (a sweet rice dish), offering it to the sun god, and celebrating with family gatherings, kolams (rangoli), and cultural performances.

Navaratri & Vijayadashami

Navaratri, celebrated in September or October, is a nine-night festival dedicated to Goddess Durga. The festival includes "kolu" (displays of dolls), prayers, cultural performances, and feasts. It culminates in Vijayadashami, which marks the victory of good over evil, and is considered an auspicious day for starting new ventures.

Krishna Jayanti (Janmashtami)

Krishna Jayanti, celebrated in August or September, marks the birth of Lord Krishna. The festival involves temple worship, devotional singing, and the "Uriyadi" tradition, where a clay pot filled with butter is hung high, and groups of people try to break it. It celebrates the playful spirit of Krishna's childhood.
