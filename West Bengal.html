Durga Puja

Durga Puja, celebrated in September or October, is the most important and widely celebrated festival in West Bengal. It honors Goddess Durgaâ€™s victory over the buffalo demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. The festival is marked by grand processions, elaborate decorations, cultural performances, and prayers. The city of Kolkata (Calcutta) comes alive during Durga Puja with beautiful pandals (temporary structures) showcasing stunning artistic displays, and people actively participate in prayers, feasts, and celebrations.

Poila Boishakh (Bengali New Year)

Poila Boishakh, celebrated in April, marks the Bengali New Year. It is a time for families to come together, wear new clothes, and offer prayers for a prosperous year ahead. The festival is celebrated with feasts, traditional sweets like "Rosogolla" and "Misti Doi" (sweetened yogurt), and cultural events like dances and music. Businesses also open their new accounting books on this day, symbolizing a fresh start.

Eid-ul-Fitr

Eid-ul-Fitr, celebrated in April or May, marks the end of Ramadan, the Islamic holy month of fasting. It is widely observed by the Muslim community in West Bengal with prayers at mosques, feasts, and giving of charity. Special dishes like "Sheer Khurma" (a sweet dish made with milk and dates) are prepared, and families come together to celebrate with gifts and festive meals.
