Bihar

Chhath Puja

Chhath Puja, celebrated in October or November, is the most important and unique festival of Bihar. Dedicated to the Sun God and his consort Usha, it is a festival of thanksgiving for the blessings of life and nature. People observe rigorous fasting, offer prayers to the Sun God at riverbanks, and make offerings like "Thekua" (a traditional sweet). The celebrations, involving rituals like standing in water while offering prayers, are highly significant and widely observed in Bihar, particularly in Patna, the state capital.

Sama Chakeva

Sama Chakeva, celebrated in November or December, is a unique festival observed in Bihar, especially by the women of Mithilanchal. It marks the end of the winter season and the arrival of the harvest. The festival involves making clay idols of birds like Sama and Chakeva and offering prayers for the well-being of loved ones. Women sing folk songs, decorate their homes, and celebrate with family and friends.

Diwali

Diwali, celebrated in October or November, is widely observed in Bihar with immense fervor. It marks the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after his victory over Ravana. The festival is celebrated with the lighting of oil lamps (diyas), bursting of crackers, and worship of Goddess Lakshmi for wealth and prosperity. The city of Patna, in particular, is beautifully illuminated during the festival.
