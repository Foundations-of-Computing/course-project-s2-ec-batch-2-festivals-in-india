Chhattisgarh

Teej

Teej, celebrated in August or September, is particularly observed by women in Chhattisgarh. It is a festival that marks the monsoon season and symbolizes marital bliss and well-being. Women fast and dress in bright clothes, sing folk songs, and swing on trees, hoping for prosperity and harmony in their marriages.

Gudi Padwa

Gudi Padwa, celebrated in March or April, is the Chhattisgarh New Year. It marks the beginning of the spring season and is celebrated by setting up a "Gudi" (a flag with a pot) outside homes. People prepare a special dish called "Puran Poli" and participate in prayers and cultural events.

Bastar Dussehra

Bastar Dussehra, celebrated in September or October, is one of the unique and grandest festivals in Chhattisgarh. Unlike other regions, Bastar Dussehra is celebrated for 75 days in the Bastar region, where tribal customs and rituals are performed. The festival honors Goddess Danteshwari and involves grand processions, traditional dances, and a ceremonial procession with a wooden idol of the goddess
